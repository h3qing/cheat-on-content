## Post (primary)

For 20 years, career advice was: find your weakness, grind it to "fine."

Round yourself out. Don't let the gap drag you down. Be the well-balanced operator.

That math only worked when you had to do everything yourself.

AI broke the math.

A weakness is now cheap to fill. Bad at writing, slides, SQL, a second language? That's a prompt away from "good enough." The thing you used to spend a quarter dragging from a 3 to a 6 — AI does to a 7 while you sleep.

A real strength is the opposite. It doesn't average out. It compounds.

So the ROI flipped:
→ Fixing a weakness buys you, at best, mediocre.
→ Multiplying a strength buys you an edge nobody can copy by prompting.

The new game isn't "no weaknesses." Everyone's weaknesses look the same now — patched by the same models. What's scarce is the one thing you're freakishly good at, the taste and judgment that doesn't come out of a context window.

Stop repairing your armor. Sharpen your one best weapon — and let AI carry the rest.

What's the strength you keep apologizing for not "rounding out"? That's probably the whole game.
