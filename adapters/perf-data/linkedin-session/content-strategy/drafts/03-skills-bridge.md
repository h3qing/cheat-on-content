---

60–70% of the analyst job — cleaning, transforming, charting — is automating.

If you're a student grinding to become "good at SQL and dashboards," you're training for the half of the job that's disappearing.

Here's what I'd learn instead in 2026:

1. **Framing problems, not running queries.** The agent can write the query. It can't decide which question is worth asking. That judgment is the job now.

2. **Evals.** When an AI does the analysis, your value is knowing when it's wrong. Learn to test outputs, not just produce them.

3. **One real domain.** "Generalist analyst" is the most automatable role there is. Pick a domain (finance, growth, ops, healthcare) and go deep enough to smell a wrong number.

4. **Shipping, not notebooks.** The people winning right now build small things that work end-to-end, not perfect analyses nobody uses.

The tools got cheap. Taste, judgment, and domain depth didn't.
