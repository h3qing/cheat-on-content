I used code to categorize all 4,169 of my LinkedIn connections.

The result surprised me:

- 82% are senior — professionals, directors, founders
- 1.9% are students
- The rest: recruiters, educators, a handful of creators

Here's the thing I didn't expect: **your network is your distribution.** LinkedIn shows every post to a slice of your 1st-degree first, then amplifies only if they engage fast.

So if your connections are 82% senior, your *beginner* content quietly dies — not because it's bad, but because the room it's shown to first isn't the audience for it.

I'd been wondering why my "how to get started" posts underperformed my "here's how the field is changing" posts. Now I know. I was posting to the wrong room.

So I'm rebalancing on purpose: accepting the wave of students applying to our roles, and writing more that serves them. Early data says it's working — one batch roughly doubled my under-30-career share.

The meta-lesson: you can't grow an audience you can't see. Measure who's actually there first.
